  PART 1: MODEL ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

  Type:               3-Layer Dual XGBoost + Monte Carlo Simulation
  Layer 1 (BF):       Opportunity model — predicts batters faced (20 features)
  Layer 2 (K/BF):     Skill/matchup model — predicts K rate per batter (37 features)
  Layer 3:            Monte Carlo simulation (10K sims) for K distribution & prop probs
  Final Prediction:   pred_BF x pred_K_per_BF + full probability distribution
  Starter Filter:     Only pitchers with sportsbook K prop lines (confirmed starters)
  Target Variables:   BF residual (actual - baseline) and K/BF residual (actual - season rate)
  Baseline Formula:   0.50 * season_avg + 0.30 * rolling_5 + 0.20 * rolling_3

  Training Data:      4,062 starts (2024-03-29 to 2024-09-16)

    min_child_weight: 8

    Simulations:      10,000 per prediction

NOTABLE FEATURE INSIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. market_k_line is #2 in the KBF model and #4 in the BF model. The
     sportsbook line encodes lineup information, weather, umpire tendencies,
     and other signals we don't have direct access to. It acts as a powerful
     "wisdom of the crowd" anchor.

  2. two_strike_putaway_rate (#4 in KBF) is more important than raw whiff_rate
     (#23). This makes sense — the ability to finish at-bats in two-strike
     counts directly creates strikeouts.

  3. k_minus_bb_rate (#3 in KBF) outranks opp_k_rate (#5). Pitcher command
     (K-BB differential) matters more than opponent K tendency.

  4. All 4 pitch x lineup interaction features are meaningful (ranks 14-15,
     21, 35), confirming the arsenal-matchup approach adds value.

  5. tto_k_decay (#18) shows time-through-order effects matter. Pitchers
     who maintain K rates in later ABs have a real advantage.

  6. The 4 odds features (market_k_line, implied_team_win_prob, team_moneyline,
     game_total_line) represent 17.2% of total BF importance and 6.0% of KBF
     importance. Market data is a substantial information source.

PRE-SEASON PREPARATION (March 2025)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Collect 2025 spring training / early season data as it becomes available
  2. Keep the 2024 model as baseline — do NOT retrain until sufficient 2025 data
  3. Begin collecting live odds daily via the-odds-api.com
  4. Set up paper trading tracker


DATA COLLECTION PHASE (March 28 — May 31)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Collect all pitcher game stats, Statcast data, odds, K prop lines daily
  - Run model predictions but DO NOT BET
  - Paper trade only — track model predictions vs outcomes
  - Target: accumulate ~1,300+ starts before considering real bets
  - Monitor model MAE vs market K line MAE on a rolling 2-week basis


VALIDATION PHASE (June 1 — June 14)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - By June 1, you should have ~1,300-1,500 training starts
  - Retrain model on 2025 data
  - Run 2-week paper trade validation
  - CHECK: Is model MAE beating market K line MAE?
  - CHECK: Is unders-at-3.5 win rate above 55%?
  - CHECK: Is yield positive on paper trades?
  - If all three pass → proceed to live betting
  - If not → continue paper trading, recheck every 2 weeks


LIVE BETTING RULES (June 15 onwards)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  WHAT TO BET:
    - UNDERS ONLY (do not bet overs)
    - Lines 3.5 and 4.5 ONLY (do not bet 5.5, 6.5, 7.5)
    - Only when model shows positive EV

  BANKROLL MANAGEMENT:
    - Starting bankroll: Your chosen amount
    - Use 0.25 Kelly (quarter Kelly) sizing
    - Cap any single bet at 10% of current bankroll
    - Minimum bet: $1 or your book's minimum

  EXPECTED VOLUME:
    - ~5-15 bets per day on a full slate
    - ~200-400 bets per month during regular season

  EXPECTED PERFORMANCE (based on 2024 walk-forward):
    - Win rate: 54-58%
    - Yield: +5% to +18% per dollar wagered
    - Monthly variance: some losing weeks are normal
    - Max drawdown: plan for up to 40% drawdown


DANGER ZONES — WHEN TO PAUSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. ALL-STAR BREAK (mid-July)
     July was the worst month in 2024 walk-forward. Roster shuffles,
     trade deadline uncertainty, and second-half adjustments hurt the model.
     RECOMMENDATION: Reduce bet sizing by 50% from July 10 — August 1.

  2. TRADE DEADLINE (late July)
     Pitcher trades change team contexts that the model hasn't learned yet.
     RECOMMENDATION: Pause betting for 3-5 days after major trades.

  3. SEPTEMBER CALLUPS
     Expanded rosters in September change bullpen dynamics.
     The model actually performed well in September 2024, but monitor closely.

  4. THREE CONSECUTIVE LOSING DAYS
     If you have 3 straight days of net losses, pause for 1 day and review.
     Check if the model is making systematic errors (e.g., a weather factor).

  5. DRAWDOWN > 30%
     If bankroll drops 30% from peak, cut bet sizing in half until recovery.
     If drawdown reaches 50%, stop and reassess the model.
